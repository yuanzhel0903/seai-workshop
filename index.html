About SEAI

Recent advances in Embodied AI have improved navigation and manipulation, yet these gains often fail to transfer to social environments where success depends on human-robot interaction, coordination, and social intention anticipation. Our workshop establishes Social Embodied AI as an agenda for developing and evaluating agents that perceive, reason about, and act on social context in human-robot interaction and collaboration. We focus on three core gaps: simulated and physical environments that expose communicative interfaces and measurable social signals, learning targets that support intention and belief-aware reasoning and partner adaptation under partial observability, and process-oriented evaluation that distinguishes socially compliant and safe behavior beyond task success.

Key Challenges We Aim to Address

Challenge 1: Intention and Belief Understanding
How can embodied agents infer human intentions, beliefs, and emotional states from sparse multimodal cues? This challenge addresses predicting partner goals online, representing epistemic gaps and false beliefs, modeling goal hierarchies that enable social replanning, and estimating affective states to adapt timing and assistance.
Challenge 2: Collaborative Adaptation
How can agents build persistent partner models and adapt to complement human collaborators? This involves estimating what information partners can access, inferring shifting roles during joint activities, predicting complementary actions that divide labor effectively, and learning from repeated interactions to enable personalized coordination.
Challenge 3: Social Norms and Affective Awareness
How can agents recognize and respect context-dependent social norms and individual preferences? This encompasses modeling engagement to support well-timed interventions, adapting to cultural conventions and unwritten social rules, and personalizing behaviors based on user-specific needs, privacy expectations, and spatial interaction preferences.

Call for Papers

The 1st Workshop on Social Embodied AI @ ICML 2026 invites submissions presenting innovative research at the intersection of embodied AI, human-robot interaction, and social intelligence. We welcome work that addresses core challenges in developing agents that perceive, reason about, and act on social context in embodied settings. Submissions may introduce new models, datasets, benchmarks, simulation environments, or real-world applications.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to: intention and belief understanding (human intention prediction, belief state inference, goal hierarchy modeling, emotional state inference), collaborative adaptation (knowledge and awareness estimation, role inference, complementary action prediction, interaction history modeling), social norms and affective awareness (attention and engagement modeling, social norm recognition, preference adaptation, privacy-aware behaviors), simulation environments and benchmarks for social embodied AI, process-oriented evaluation metrics, datasets for social human-robot interaction, and safety and social compliance evaluation.

Submission Site

Abstracts and papers will be submitted through OpenReview (link TBD).

Submission Tracks

  • Archival Track: Submissions to this track will be considered for publication in the official ICML 2026 workshop proceedings. Papers should be between 4 to 8 pages in length for the main text, formatted according to the ICML 2026 paper submission guidelines. There is no page limit for references or appendices. Selected papers will be presented in an oral session.
  • Non-Archival Track: We invite submissions of ongoing projects, preliminary results, or work published elsewhere (e.g., on arXiv or at other conferences). Submissions should be limited to 4 pages (as extended abstracts), formatted according to the ICML 2026 paper submission guidelines. These papers will not be included in the official proceedings but may be selected for poster or oral presentations.

Submission Guidelines

Format:  All submissions must be a single PDF file formatted according to the ICML 2026 paper submission guidelines. The main text must be self-contained. Reviewers are not required to read beyond the main text. References and appendices are not included in the page limit.

Review Process:  Each submission will undergo a rigorous double-blind review by at least three reviewers. Conflicts of interest will be managed through the OpenReview platform.

Program Committee:  A diverse committee of experts from machine learning, computer vision, robotics, human-robot interaction, and social intelligence will serve as reviewers. The full list will be announced on the workshop website.

Double-blind reviewing:  All submissions must be anonymized and may not contain any identifying information that may violate the double-blind reviewing policy. This policy applies to any supplementary or linked material as well, including code. If you are including links to any external material, it is your responsibility to guarantee anonymous browsing. Please do not include acknowledgements at submission time.

Contact:  For any questions, please contact us at yuanzhel@seas.upenn.edu or xucao@pediamed.ai.
